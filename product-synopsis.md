# LexOps Firm Console

LexOps Firm Console is a law-firm AI paralegal workspace for mock plaintiff-side personal injury teams. It turns case files, medical records, discovery, transcripts, and attorney notes into organized work product that attorneys can review, revise, and serve.

## Product Options

1. Web app

The primary firm workspace. Best for matter dashboards, document upload, review queues, drafting, audit logs, permissions, and admin controls.

2. Mobile companion

The attorney and paralegal field app. Best for quick matter lookup, voice notes, deposition prep, review alerts, client-call summaries, and task capture from court, mediation, or travel.

3. Private deployment or hardware appliance

The higher-security option. Best for firms that want dedicated infrastructure, firm-controlled storage, local processing options, stricter data retention, and stronger separation from shared cloud environments.

## Core Capabilities

- Matter command center with case posture, parties, deadlines, missing evidence, and draft status.
- Discovery drafting and response support for interrogatories, RFPs, RFAs, objections, and attorney review notes.
- Meet-and-confer and motion-to-compel support with deficiencies, missing materials, and deadline risk flags.
- Medical record review with chronologies, provider summaries, objective findings, treatment gaps, prior injury issues, and missing records.
- Deposition review with page-line summaries, admissions, contradictions, impeachment issues, and follow-up discovery.
- Demand and mediation support with liability, damages, specials, future care issues, bad facts, and evidence gaps.
- Matter-scoped memory so LexOps remembers the case without blending facts across matters.
- Draft-only external communication controls so attorneys approve before anything is sent, filed, or served.
- Role-based access, audit trail, and retention policy foundations for confidential legal and medical information.

## Security Topics For The Next Walkthrough

- Hosting model: shared SaaS, dedicated tenant, VPC, or on-prem/private appliance.
- Encryption at rest and in transit.
- Role-based access by firm, matter, user, and task.
- Audit logs for uploads, generated drafts, approvals, exports, and deletions.
- Matter-scoped memory and data retention controls.
- Privacy, PHI, and client-identifiable information handling.
- Business Associate Agreement expectations if protected health information is stored or processed.
- Secure document ingestion, malware scanning, redaction, and access expiration.
